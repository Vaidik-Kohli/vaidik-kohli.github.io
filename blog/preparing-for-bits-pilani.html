Preparing for BITS Pilani — My Strategy

Education BITSAT Strategy

Balancing coding, freelancing, and competitive exam preparation isn't easy. But with the right strategy and mindset, it's absolutely doable. Here's my approach to getting into BITS Pilani Goa for Computer Science.

Why BITS Pilani?

BITS Pilani has always been on my radar. It's one of the few top-tier Indian institutions that truly values practical skills alongside academics. The Goa campus, in particular, has an incredible CS program and a vibrant tech culture with clubs, hackathons, and startup ecosystems.

What excites me most is the freedom BITS gives you. No attendance pressure, the Practice School system for real industry experience, and a community of incredibly motivated peers. For someone like me who already freelances and builds things, this environment feels perfect.

The BITSAT Challenge

BITSAT is a unique exam. It's fast-paced (150 questions in 3 hours), covers Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, English, and Logical Reasoning. The key isn't just knowing the material — it's speed and accuracy.

My preparation pillars:

  • NCERTs first — Building a rock-solid foundation from textbooks
  • Practice problems — Solving 50+ problems daily across subjects
  • Mock tests — Weekly full-length timed tests to build speed
  • Error analysis — Reviewing every wrong answer to find patterns

The Balancing Act

Here's the hard part: I don't want to stop coding while preparing. Development and design are what I'm passionate about. Completely pausing them for 2 years would be counterproductive — I'd lose momentum, skills, and motivation.

My solution is time-blocking:

  • Morning (6-8 AM) — Mathematics practice (when my mind is sharpest)
  • School hours — Regular academics
  • Afternoon (4-7 PM) — Physics and Chemistry
  • Evening (8-10 PM) — Coding / freelance work (this is my reward)
  • Weekends — Mock tests + project work
"You don't have to choose between your passion and your goals. You just have to be intentional about how you spend your time."

What I've Learned So Far

Three months into this strategy, here's what I've realized:

Consistency beats intensity. Studying 4 focused hours beats 8 distracted ones. I use the Pomodoro technique — 45 minutes of deep focus, 10 minutes break.

Coding actually helps with exams. The problem-solving mindset from programming directly transfers to math and physics. Breaking down complex problems into smaller steps is the same skill in both worlds.

Health matters. I try to play cricket 2-3 times a week, sleep 7+ hours, and avoid doom-scrolling. Physical and mental health are the foundation everything else is built on.

The Roadmap Ahead

BITSAT 2028 is still a couple of years away, and I plan to use every month wisely. By the time I sit for the exam, I want to have not just the scores — but also a portfolio of real projects, freelance experience, and a strong GitHub profile.

Because at the end of the day, getting into BITS is the goal — but becoming a great developer is the mission. And the two aren't mutually exclusive.
